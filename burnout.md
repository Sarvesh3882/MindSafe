## WHAT IT MEASURES
Burnout is defined by the WHO (ICD-11, 2019) as:
"A syndrome conceptualized as resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed."

Three dimensions (MBI):
1. Emotional Exhaustion — feeling emotionally overextended and drained by work
2. Depersonalization — cynical, detached, or impersonal response to work or people
3. Reduced Personal Accomplishment — feeling incompetent, ineffective, or unproductive

Burnout = HIGH exhaustion + HIGH depersonalization + LOW personal accomplishment

## OFFICIAL QUESTIONS — MBI Human Services Survey (MBI-HSS)
Instructions: "How often do you have these feelings?"
Response options (frequency): 0 = Never | 1 = A few times/year | 2 = Once a month or less
| 3 = A few times/month | 4 = Once a week | 5 = A few times/week | 6 = Every day

EMOTIONAL EXHAUSTION SUBSCALE (9 items):
EE1.  I feel emotionally drained from my work.
EE2.  I feel used up at the end of the workday.
EE3.  I feel fatigued when I get up in the morning and have to face another day on the job.
EE4.  Working with people all day is really a strain for me.
EE5.  I feel burned out from my work.
EE6.  I feel frustrated by my job.
EE7.  I feel I'm working too hard on my job.
EE8.  Working with people directly puts too much stress on me.
EE9.  I feel like I'm at the end of my rope.

DEPERSONALIZATION SUBSCALE (5 items):
DP1.  I feel I treat some recipients as if they were impersonal objects.
DP2.  I've become more callous toward people since I took this job.
DP3.  I worry that this job is hardening me emotionally.
DP4.  I don't really care what happens to some recipients.
DP5.  I feel that recipients blame me for some of their problems.

PERSONAL ACCOMPLISHMENT SUBSCALE (8 items — reverse interpretation):
PA1.  I can easily understand how my recipients feel about things.
PA2.  I deal very effectively with the problems of my recipients.
PA3.  I feel I'm positively influencing other people's lives through my work.
PA4.  I feel very energetic.
PA5.  I can easily create a relaxed atmosphere with my recipients.
PA6.  I feel exhilarated after working closely with my recipients.
PA7.  I have accomplished many worthwhile things in this job.
PA8.  In my work, I deal with emotional problems very calmly.

Note: For General Survey (MBI-GS — applicable to all professions):
"Recipients" becomes "work" and subscales become Exhaustion, Cynicism, and Efficacy.
MBI-GS (16 items) is more widely used outside health/human services settings.

## SCORING
Each subscale is scored independently — subscales should NOT be combined into one total.
High scores on Exhaustion and Depersonalization + Low scores on Personal Accomplishment = Burnout

Subscale     | Low          | Moderate    | High
-------------|--------------|-------------|-------
Exhaustion   | 0–16         | 17–26       | 27–54
Depersonalization| 0–6      | 7–12        | 13–30
Pers. Accomplish.| 39–48 (high good)| 32–38 | 0–31 (low = worse)

Note: Burnout interpretation requires looking at all three subscales together,
not any single number. The MBI is not a simple pass/fail test.

## WHO OFFICIAL DEFINITION (ICD-11, 2019)
Burnout is characterized by three dimensions:
1. Feelings of energy depletion or exhaustion
2. Increased mental distance from one's job, or feelings of negativism or cynicism related to one's job
3. Reduced professional efficacy

ICD-11 code: QD85 — Burnout
Important: WHO classifies burnout as an occupational phenomenon, NOT a medical disorder.
Source: https://www.who.int/news/item/28-05-2019-burn-out-an-occupational-phenomenon-international-classification-of-diseases

## PSYCHOMETRIC PROPERTIES
- Internal consistency (Cronbach's α): 0.85–0.89 per subscale (Maslach & Jackson, 1981)
- Validated across professions: healthcare, education, IT, finance, government, students
- Used in 40+ years of organizational research globally
- MBI-GS validated across 8 countries in cross-cultural nursing study (PMC2700194)
- Burnout correlates with: depression (r ≈ 0.50+), anxiety, sleep disturbance, cardiovascular risk
- Distinguished from depression: burnout is work/role-specific; depression is pervasive across contexts

## GLOBAL & INDIA EPIDEMIOLOGY
Global:
- WHO classified burnout in ICD-11 in 2019 — first official global recognition
- 39% of the global public health workforce reports burnout (systematic review, n = 215,787; PMC11110232)
- 48% of global desk-based workers report burnout (Boston Consulting Group, 2023, n = 11,000)
- 52% of all workers globally feel burned out (Indeed, 2021 — +9% from pre-COVID)
- Over 40% of workers in 6 countries report burnout; Gen Z (18–29): 48% affected (Future Forum, 2022)
- Women (46%) report burnout more than men (37%) (Future Forum, 2022)
- Gallup (2023): 44% of workers experienced daily stress — record high since tracking began
- Workplace stress costs the US economy $300–500 billion/year (APA + American Institute of Stress)
- Burnout contributes to ~120,000 deaths and $190 billion in healthcare spending/year (US estimate)
- Global healthcare alone: nurses burnout prevalence 11.23% across 49 countries (Woo et al.)
  General practitioners: 37% burnout globally

India:
- India's burnout rate is above the global average (BCG Report, 2023)
- 30% of Indian employees report daily stress (Gallup State of Global Workplace)
- South Asia: highest daily stress and anger rates of any world region (Gallup)
- 84% of Indian corporate employees report low mood or depressive thoughts (HCL Healthcare)
- 45% of Indian employees experience Sunday evening anxiety about work (YourDOST, 2024)
- Indian medical students: 37.2% suicidal ideation (2023, north India survey, PMC10793817)
- IT sector, healthcare, banking, and teaching are India's highest-burnout professions

## KEY SIGNALS FOR SAATHI TO DETECT
- Saying "I'm so exhausted but I can't stop working"
- Loss of passion for work or studies that were once meaningful
- Feeling detached, going through the motions, emotionally numb toward their role
- Cynical comments: "what's the point," "nothing I do matters," "this place is toxic"
- Feeling ineffective despite working very hard
- Physical exhaustion that doesn't resolve with rest or weekends
- Dreading going to work / college
- Difficulty feeling emotions related to work — neither satisfaction nor distress
- Increasing irritability, sarcasm, or withdrawal from colleagues / classmates

## SAATHI BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES

DO:
- Acknowledge: "That kind of exhaustion — where rest doesn't help — is really real."
- Normalize burnout: globally recognized, very common especially in high-pressure environments
- Use the word "burnout" only if the person uses it first or the signals are unambiguous
- Invite reflection: "When did you last do something just for yourself?"
- Suggest one small recovery action: a walk, a break from screens, one enjoyable activity
- If prolonged: strongly suggest professional support or Tele-MANAS
- Ask about support systems: "Is there someone you trust that you can talk to?"

DO NOT:
- Tell them to "push through it" or "be stronger"
- Suggest productivity hacks or time management tips (worsens exhaustion)
- Say "everyone's stressed" (minimizing)
- Bombard with suggestions
- Immediately label it as burnout or a mental health disorder
