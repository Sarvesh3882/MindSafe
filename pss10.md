## WHAT IT MEASURES
The PSS-10 measures the degree to which a person perceives life situations
as unpredictable, uncontrollable, and overloading over the past month.
Informed by Lazarus & Folkman's Transactional Model of Stress (1984).
It is the most widely cited psychological instrument for measuring perceived stress,
with 30,000+ citations since 1983.

## OFFICIAL QUESTIONS (Exact Wording)
Instructions: "The questions in this scale ask you about your feelings and thoughts during the last month.
In each case, you will be asked to indicate how often you felt or thought a certain way."
Response options: 0 = Never | 1 = Almost never | 2 = Sometimes | 3 = Fairly often | 4 = Very often

Q1.  In the last month, how often have you been upset because of something that happened unexpectedly?
Q2.  In the last month, how often have you felt that you were unable to control the important things in your life?
Q3.  In the last month, how often have you felt nervous and stressed?
Q4.  In the last month, how often have you felt confident about your ability to handle your personal problems?  *(reverse scored)*
Q5.  In the last month, how often have you felt that things were going your way?  *(reverse scored)*
Q6.  In the last month, how often have you found that you could not cope with all the things that you had to do?
Q7.  In the last month, how often have you been able to control irritations in your life?  *(reverse scored)*
Q8.  In the last month, how often have you felt that you were on top of things?  *(reverse scored)*
Q9.  In the last month, how often have you been angered because of things that were outside of your control?
Q10. In the last month, how often have you felt difficulties were piling up so high that you could not overcome them?

## SCORING
Scoring: Reverse-score Q4, Q5, Q7, Q8 (i.e., 0→4, 1→3, 2→2, 3→1, 4→0)
Total score = Sum of all 10 items (Range: 0–40)
Higher scores = Higher perceived stress

Score  | Stress Level      | Interpretation
-------|-------------------|------------------------------------------
0–13   | Low stress        | Normal functioning; monitor periodically
14–26  | Moderate stress   | Support coping strategies; follow up
27–40  | High perceived stress | Significant distress; recommend professional support

Two subscales:
1. Perceived Helplessness (Q1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 10) — feelings of lack of control
2. Perceived Self-Efficacy (Q4, 5, 7, 8) — perceived ability to cope

## PSYCHOMETRIC PROPERTIES
- Internal consistency (Cronbach's α): > 0.70 in all 12 validated studies (Lee, 2012 review)
- Predictive validity falls after 4–8 weeks (reflects recent subjective stress appraisal)
- Validated across: adolescents (age 12+), adults, older adults, diverse ethnic groups
- Used for: community screening, clinical research, workplace wellness, academic studies
- Higher PSS-10 scores associated with: elevated cortisol, suppressed immune function,
  higher markers of biological aging, increased cardiovascular risk
- PSS-10 superior to PSS-14 and PSS-4 in psychometric properties (Lee, 2012; Taylor, 2015)
- Gold-standard instrument for perceived stress; cited >30,000 times

## GLOBAL & INDIA EPIDEMIOLOGY
Global:
- Stress is recognized as a major contributor to the global mental health burden
- WHO World Mental Health Report 2022: One in eight people globally lives with a mental disorder,
  with stress-related disorders among the most prevalent
  Source: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240049338
- Gallup State of the Global Workplace 2023: 44% of workers experienced daily stress — record high
  (up from 38% pre-COVID)
  Source: https://www.gallup.com/workplace/349484/state-of-the-global-workplace.aspx
- COVID-19 caused a 26% increase in depression and 28% increase in anxiety globally (WHO, 2021)
- Workplace stress costs the US economy $300–500 billion annually (APA; American Institute of Stress)

India:
- 30% of Indian employees report experiencing daily stress (Gallup, State of Global Workplace)
- South Asia (including India) has the highest percentage of employees experiencing daily stress
  and anger of any region globally (Gallup Report)
- 84% of Indian corporate employees report low mood or depressive thoughts (HCL Healthcare Report)
- 59% of Indian corporate employees show signs of moderate to severe anxiety (HCL Healthcare Report)
- 45% of Indian employees experience anxiety every Sunday evening about returning to work
  (YourDOST Emotional Wellness Survey, 2024)
- India's burnout rate is above the global average (Boston Consulting Group, 2023, n = 11,000)
- Nearly 50% of Indian employees are actively looking for a new job — largely stress-driven (Gallup)

## KEY SIGNALS FOR SAATHI TO DETECT
- Saying they feel overwhelmed, out of control, or like "too much is happening at once"
- Difficulty keeping up with responsibilities at work, college, or home
- Feeling like problems are "piling up" with no way out
- Irritability or anger about small things that feel outside their control
- Saying "I don't know how to manage everything"
- Feeling that nothing is going their way
- Physical stress signals: headaches, tension, stomach issues (mentioned in passing)
- Saying they feel stuck and helpless even though nothing catastrophic has happened

## SAATHI BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES

DO:
- Validate the feeling of overwhelm: "That sounds like a lot to carry all at once."
- Normalize: "Feeling overwhelmed is really common, especially when demands keep coming."
- Help them identify one thing they CAN control (even something tiny)
- Suggest: break the problem into small steps; prioritize just one thing today
- Offer a breathing or grounding technique (see techniques.md)
- If high perceived stress is persistent → gently suggest talking to a counselor or calling Tele-MANAS

DO NOT:
- Tell them to "manage their time better" or "just prioritize"
- Project urgency — stay calm and grounding
- Use the words "PSS-10," "stress score," or clinical framing
- List too many suggestions at once (overwhelming someone who is already overwhelmed)
