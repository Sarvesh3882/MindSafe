## 1. Legal and Regulatory Basis

### 1.1 Supreme Court Direction

In pursuance of the judgment of the Supreme Court of India dated 25.07.2025 in **C.A. (Crl.) No. 3177/2025 — Sukdeb Saha v. The State of Andhra Pradesh**, the Government of India was instructed to frame a **Uniform Mental Health Policy for Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs)** in India on the basis of:

- UMMEED Guidelines
- Manodarpan Initiative
- National Suicide Prevention Strategy

The court's order underscores the implementation challenges in ensuring that policies translate into **evidence-based practices**. Accordingly, an expert committee was constituted by the UGC to frame the Uniform Mental Health and Well-being Policy for HEIs in India.

*[Source: Chapter 1, UGC Guidelines on Uniform Policy on Mental Health & Well-being for HEIs, January 2026]*

### 1.2 UGC Regulatory Provisions

- UGC (Institution Deemed to be University) Regulations, 2019 — Sub-clause 10.07.1 and 10.07.2
- UGC (Institution Deemed to be University) Regulations, 2023 — Clause 11(3), 11(4), 13(1), 13(2)
- UGC Guidelines for Promotion of Physical Fitness, Sports, Student's Health, Welfare, Psychological and Emotional Well-being at HEIs — 13 April 2023
- UGC Guidelines on Uniform Policy on Mental Health & Well-being for HEIs — January 2026 (approved at the 594th UGC Meeting)

### 1.3 National Mental Health Context

*(As cited in UGC Uniform Policy, Chapter 1, Section 1.1)*

- In India, **1 in 10 people** (approximately 10.6% of the population) suffer from mental health conditions
- **7.3% of youth aged 18–29 years** suffer from severe psychiatric conditions
- The Indian crude **suicide rate for ages 15–29 years** is 15.72 per 100,000 population
- According to the National Crime Records Bureau, **7.6% of deaths by suicide** are reported among the student population
- Prevalence of mental health crises among students in HEIs ranges from substance use, mood and anxiety disorders, to self-harm behaviours

### 1.4 Key National Initiatives Referenced

- **MANODARPAN**: Ministry of Education initiative providing psychological support to students, families, and teachers via a national toll-free helpline, a directory of available mental health professionals, and regular live interactive sessions (Sahyog and Paricharcha)
- **UMMEED** (Understand, Motivate, Manage, Empathise, Empower, Develop): Ministry of Education guidelines for mental health in educational institutions
- **NEP 2020**: Mandates that learning environments be conducive to students' flourishing, providing access to reliable, safe, and stigma-free counselling services by licensed mental health professionals
- **National Suicide Prevention Strategy**: Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, 2022
- **TeleMANAS**: Tele Mental Health Assistance and Networking Across States — national tele mental health program under Ministry of Health and Family Welfare
- **Malaviya Mission Teacher Training Programme**: Trains faculty to identify student mental health concerns for early intervention
- **1st National Well-being Conclave**: Held 9–10 November 2024 at IIT Hyderabad — developed actionable strategies for best practices on promoting mental health across HEIs

## 4. UGC Uniform Policy — Objectives

*[Source: UGC Guidelines on Uniform Policy on Mental Health & Well-being for HEIs, Chapter 1, Section 1.2]*

To build a robust Mental Health Ecosystem in HEIs, aligned with the **National Education Policy (NEP) 2020** and the **Vision of Viksit Bharat 2047**, in line with **Sustainable Development Goal 3** of the United Nations (to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages), a Uniform Mental Health Framework across all HEIs is essential.

The Uniform Policy on Mental Health and Well-being is designed to **prioritize student and faculty well-being as a key pillar of quality education and institutional excellence**, to achieve holistic development through a uniform mental health framework across all HEIs.

**The following are the official objectives of the UGC Guidelines:**

- Build a sustainable ecosystem of psycho-social support that integrates promotion, prevention, early detection, and intervention, for an inclusive and empathetic environment
- Ensure translation of guidelines into practice via evidence-based interventions and measurable outcomes across HEIs
- Establish a dedicated **Mental Health & Well-being Centre (MHWBC)** and **Mental Health & Well-being Monitoring Committee (MHWBMC)** to create pathways for early identification of issues and concerns for timely management
- Sensitize students, faculty, and staff for effective promotion of mental health and well-being through awareness, advocacy, and well-structured capacity-building programmes
- Ensure sustainable family-institution partnership to provide psychosocial support and establish mechanisms for linkages with community referral services
- Develop structured accountability via monitoring, compliance, and annual reporting

> **In essence, the guidelines aim to shift universities from a reactive stance to a proactive, preventive, and participatory mental health approach.**

## 5. UGC Framework — Scope and Applicability

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 1, Section 1.3]*

The provisions apply to:
- Students
- Faculty members
- Non-teaching staff

of all Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs).

The guidelines emphasize **integration across all institutional domains** — academic, administrative, residential, and extracurricular — ensuring that mental health is not confined to the Mental Health & Well-being Centre only but becomes a **shared responsibility across all stakeholders**.

## 6. UGC — Mental Health and Well-being Ecosystem (Four-Level Framework)

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 1, Section 1.4 — Figure 1: Framework of the Mental Health and Well-being Eco-system at HEIs]*

| Level | Focus | Who Is Involved | Interventions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level I** | Mental Wellbeing | MOE, UGC, HEI, MHWBMC, MHP | Implementation of Uniform Mental Health & Well-being Policy |
| **Level II** | Mental Health Knowledge, Attitudes & Behaviours | All Students, Faculty & Non-teaching Staff — Capacity Building & Awareness | Staff training in mental health promotion; Inclusive, sensitive engagement; Annual reviewing & reporting |
| **Level III** | Psychosocial Concerns — Early Identification & Intervention | Well-being Centre, Mental Health Professionals, Faculty | Life-skills training; Sensitization programs; Screening and management |
| **Level IV** | Severe Problems / Mental Disorders — Crisis Management & Protection | Mental Health Professionals & Linkages with Mental Health Service Providers | Robust reporting & redressal systems; Anti-suicide safety measures; Immediate crisis referral support; Maintaining ethical practices; Liaising with tertiary care facilities |

## 7. UGC — Roles and Responsibilities

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 2 — Building the System: Framework, Roles and Responsibilities]*

### 7.1 Roles and Responsibilities of UGC

*(Section 2.1)*

The UGC has the integral role of monitoring implementation of regulated mental health policies, identifying strengths and challenges in their adoption among HEIs, and reviewing modifications annually. It will support continuous capacity building. The UGC shall ensure strict compliance of the Uniform Policy on Mental Health & Well-being in all HEIs.

**Specific UGC responsibilities:**

- Monitor implementation through the dedicated portal **MANAS-SETU**
- Review qualitative and quantitative data via feedback and annual reports submitted to UGC to determine tangible realization of objectives
- Collaborate with NRF, ICMR, ICSSR, WHO and other Mental Health Establishments in promoting research and developing evidence-based interventions
- Recognize and disseminate evidence-based interventions and best practices from HEIs to guide mental health initiatives

### 7.2 Roles of Higher Educational Institutions (HEIs)

*(Section 2.2)*

The responsibility of HEIs is to ensure:
- Identification and optimal use of existing resources provided by ministries and UGC (helpline numbers, funds, etc.)
- Execution of guidelines to create an ecosystem of positive mental health by creating Mental Health & Well-being Centres
- Recording, reporting, and addressing any incidents of harassment and discrimination
- Creating self-development programmes and developing life skills
- Establishing standard procedures for addressing crises and emergencies
- Devising initiatives to engage family members as active nurturers of their wards

**Specific HEI obligations under the UGC Uniform Policy:**

a. Establish a dedicated **Mental Health & Well-being Centre** with all required physical infrastructure, including:
   - Dedicated rooms/spaces providing privacy while making or during waiting for appointments
   - Facilities for documentation of sessions (coded/anonymized for storage)
   - Clinical records maintained and destroyed as per HEI norms after 1 year of the student graduating
   - Contact details of the Centre and MHPs visibly displayed throughout the campus and on the website

b. Constitute a **Mental Health & Well-being Monitoring Committee (MHWBMC)** as per prescribed norms; display committee member details (name, designation, contact details/email) on the website

c. Depute required mental health professionals at the ratio of **1 MHP per 500 students**

d. Depute required faculty mentors at the ratio of **1 faculty mentor per 500 students**

e. Depute student peer supporters at the ratio of **1 peer supporter per 100 students**

f. Run a **24×7 helpline** for registering grievances and other psychosocial concerns

g. Interlink the **TeleMANAS** and other established helplines with their 24×7 helpline

h. Develop linkages with the **National Mental Health Programme (NMHP)** and its **District Mental Health Programme (DMHP)**

i. Ensure required funds for implementation of the Uniform Mental Health and Well-being Policy and training of mental health professionals

j. Report data on the dedicated UGC portal (MANAS-SETU) on a regular basis

k. Undertake short-term and long-term research studies on psychosocial challenges faced by stakeholders

### 7.3 Roles and Responsibilities of Mental Health & Well-being Monitoring Committee (MHWBMC)

*(Section 2.3)*

**Primary objectives:**
- Supervise implementation of the Uniform Mental Health Policy
- Ensure smooth functioning of the MHWBC within the code of ethics and legal boundaries of practice
- Collect and store anonymized data from the Centres
- Report incidents of crisis and emergencies, incidents of harassment, and management undertaken for each
- Report challenges faced and dealt with to the UGC

The committee shall **appoint a Nodal Officer** from the department to:
- Monitor its activities
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Serve as a liaison between the HEI and UGC for effective communication

## 8. UGC — Mental Health & Well-being Centre (MHWBC)

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 2, Section 2.4]*

Apart from conducting screening to identify emotional, psychological, social, academic, or behavioural concerns, the Centres will help in the **creation of an inclusive and engaging educational environment** that promotes learning and flourishing.

**Official functions of the MHWBC:**

a. Organizing **Sensitization & Awareness generation programmes** for all stakeholders

b. Conducting **capacity-building programmes** for faculty members (for example, gatekeeper training) to:
   - Identify students at risk and take appropriate immediate response
   - Encourage faculty to recognize and constructively respond to students exhibiting distress emotions and counter-productive behaviour
   - Address interpersonal dysfunction and absenteeism
   - Motivate learners
   - Help students overcome examination-related anxiety
   - Improve coping skills in faculty and staff

c. Conducting **sensitization programmes for parents** aimed at improving bonding with adult children; MHPs to be part of orientation programmes for freshers and to hold a meet-and-greet with family members

d. Conducting regular **Mental Health Surveys** to identify and manage problems related to stress, distress, and socio-emotional adjustment, and to develop life skills

e. Providing **immediate support, intervention, and referral** if required; offering psychological assessments/screening, guidance, counselling, and psychotherapy (individual, group, or family) for emotional concerns

f. Ascertaining immediate reporting of **serious concerns** to the Mental Health & Well-being Committee

g. Maintaining **anonymized records** of stakeholders; access limited to MHPs and competent authorities of the HEI; disclosure guidelines for safety/medical/legal cases determined by the concerned individual, treating MHP, head of institution, and legal advisory team

h. **Regular reporting** to the MHWBC and UGC

i. Establishing **linkages with external experts** and service providers

j. Conducting **research** while strictly adhering to the principles and ethics of conducting research in social sciences using human participants; ethical clearance from relevant research committees is necessary

k. If campus visits by MHPs are not possible, the nearest **government-aided hospital/medical colleges/Ayushman Arogya Mandirs or DMHP facilities** must be listed, highlighted, and shared among students, faculty, staff, and parents

l. **Telecounselling helplines** such as TeleMANAS and other emergency helplines (UGC Anti-Ragging Helpline, Women's Helpline) must be publicized through campus signages

## 9. UGC — Qualifications of Mental Health Professionals

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 2, Section 2.5]*

The MHWBC will primarily be attended to by Mental Health Professionals (MHPs) such as licensed psychiatrists, clinical psychologists, psychologists, or psychiatric social workers. They will be key trainers of the institute's peer support programme and offer supervision of peer supporters.

> **Minimum ratio prescribed:** 1 qualified clinical psychologist/psychologist/mental health professional per 500 students at the higher education institute level. For any educational institution where 100 or more students are enrolled, at least 1 MHP may be considered.

All MHPs should have **a minimum of 1 year of work experience with adolescents and youth**.

| Role | Minimum Qualification | Registration Body | Official Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Psychologist | M.A./M.Sc. in Psychology / Clinical / Counselling Psychology; or Ph.D. in Psychology | — | Psychosocial support, individual and group counselling |
| Clinical Psychologist | M.Phil. in Clinical Psychology | Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) | Psychological assessments, psychotherapy, supervision of counsellors |
| Psychiatric Social Worker (PSW) | M.Phil. in Psychiatric Social Work | — | Psychosocial case management, community linkage, student-family coordination, supervision of Social Work Counsellors |
| Psychiatric Nurse (Psy N) | M.Sc. in Psychiatric Nursing | Indian Nursing Council (INC) | Care counselling, case management, supervision of Nursing Counsellors |
| Psychiatrist | MD/DNB in Psychiatry | National Medical Commission (NMC) | Diagnostic confirmation, pharmacological/non-pharmacological support, medical supervision |

**Faculty member as coordinating officer:** A faculty member may be a co-ordinating officer to help the MHP with conducting awareness programmes. If an MHP is not available, the faculty's role is limited to:
- Raising alarm for any emergency
- Providing psychological first aid
- Informing the head of the department/institution/warden/family member of the student in crisis
- Preparing for further hospital referrals

## 10. UGC — Crisis Management and Suicide Prevention

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 3 — Readiness & Crisis Management Mechanism in HEIs]*

### 10.1 Suicide Prevention Infrastructure

*(Section 3.1)*

- Prepare peers, faculty, and staff to **recognize and identify signs of distress** in behaviour and communication such as abrupt changes in interaction, behaviour, or class attendance
- Provide training to peers and teaching/non-teaching staff to **approach the student at risk** and convey concern without probing, leading, or provocative statements/questions
- Report to the concerned person (Head/Faculty/Warden/In-charge or MHP) as a safeguard, and offer guidance/intervention
- Ensure **strict compliance with the crisis intervention protocol** as laid down by the guidelines, including appropriate referral to tertiary care service and communicating the emergency to the family/person of contact
- Following the crisis, provide all possible academic, social, and emotional support to facilitate restorative functionality of the individual
- Actively address all emotional reactions triggered in students/staff after such an event
- Extend support to parents: Address concerns from parents for their wards
- Designate **infrastructure/secure space** for confidential student counselling sessions
- The institution should have **adequate CCTV installation** (without violating privacy), lighting, safe spaces, and security in hostel corridors, libraries, study areas, and high-risk zones

### 10.2 Risk Assessment

*(Section 3.2)*

- Student/faculty and non-teaching staff representatives must identify and report **early warning signs** (e.g. social withdrawal, absenteeism, behavioural changes such as agitation or increased risk-taking) to the concerned in-charge
- Provide calm, empathetic listening without judgment to a student at potential risk
- Students identified at **moderate or high risk** must be immediately referred to MHP or emergency services

> **Note for Emergency Situations:** Institutes may share relevant information with mental health providers or emergency services without prior consent if there is an **imminent threat** to the student's safety.

- Helpline and emergency contact lists (including TeleMANAS) should be **publicly displayed** across campus via notice boards/institute website

### 10.3 Responding to a Crisis Situation

*(Section 3.3)*

- Inform the student's **parents** about the situation and arrange a meeting at an appropriate location (school counsellor's office or nearby hospital emergency room)
- Contact **emergency medical services** to ensure immediate care for the student if needed
- After addressing the immediate crisis, develop a **follow-up plan** with parents and student regarding medical and/or mental health services
- **Record incident details**: time, date, persons involved, and interventions taken

### 10.4 Post-Crisis Support and Reintegration

*(Section 3.4)*

- Provide structured **follow-up counselling** (minimum three sessions)
- Develop a **reintegration plan** to help the student return to academics safely
- Offer peer or faculty mentorship support
- Ensure **monitoring for recurring distress**

### 10.5 Institutional Review

*(Section 3.5)*

- Designated in-charges shall conduct a **monthly review** of all crisis cases, identifying systemic gaps and recommending training or structural reforms

## 11. UGC — Suggested Campus Activities

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 4 — Suggested Activities to Promote Mental Health and Well-being in the Campus]*

The year-long activities will focus on advocating the importance of mental health as a basic human right for all, building life skills, improving interpersonal relationships, and building resilience in the HEIs.

### 11.1 Activities for Students

*(Section 4.1)*

**For Freshers — 3–4 Workshops within the First Semester:**
- Introduce the role of the MHP/MHWBC in the campus
- Discuss themes of adjusting to a new academic environment
- Promote the practice of self-regulation
- Social skills and communication-skill-building workshops
- Teaching skills to identify distress and recognizing how to ask for help

**For Second and Third Year Students:**
- Short workshops on building and maintaining motivation to study
- Digital literacy and online safety
- Life-skills development
- Developing coping and relaxation skills
- Effective time management skills

**For Final Year Students:**
- Building resilience skills
- Communication skills to improve interpersonal functioning

**General (All Students):**
- Group sessions on life-skills and resilience building
- Handling grief
- Skills to develop self-check-ins for self-care
- Addressing normalized deviant behaviours on social media
- Addressing "psychology speak"

### 11.2 Activities for Faculty, Institutional Leaders, and Non-teaching Staff

*(Section 4.2)*

- Workshops on building resilience and coping skills in teachers, non-teaching staff, and institutional leaders
- Stress management
- Anger management/developing skills to help a hostile student
- Training in **psychological first aid**
- Building compassionate communication between the teacher and learner
- **Awareness programmes with parents every 6 months** on relevant issues

### 11.3 Mindfulness and Relaxation Sessions

*(Section 4.3)*

- Teaching mindfulness techniques and structured journaling exercises as a group activity to enhance social connectedness, feelings of gratitude, and prosocial behaviour
- Faculty and non-teaching staff, including institutional leaders, must be encouraged to engage in mindfulness and relaxation sessions
- Create spaces for quiet reflection or to practice meditation/mindfulness
- **Integrate the concept of well-being and happiness into the curriculum** via a credit-based or non-credit-based system for encouraging and promoting sustained emotional growth, self-regulation, and resilience among students

### 11.4 Awareness and Observance Days

*(Section 4.4)*

- Organise campaigns with interested students to raise awareness on the **World Mental Health Day** or **World Suicide Prevention Day** theme for the specific year
- Conduct a workshop on identifying character strengths and practicing a strength every day for a week
- Arrange **group competitions** (art/essay/poster making, etc.) within students to help bond with peers beyond the friends group
- Lectures and discussions on rights, responsibilities, and laws around mental health, information and digital protection, harassment; awareness and advocacy for helplines and steps for their utilization

## 12. UGC — Data Reporting and Accountability

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 5 — Data Reporting to UGC and Accountability]*

Each HEI must submit a regular mental health and well-being report to the UGC on **MANAS-SETU Portal** containing **only anonymized data**. Any personal identifying information of students must be stored securely in an **encrypted format** and access restricted exclusively to authorized personnel of the Mental Health and Well-being Center.

**The report must include data on the following (as specified by UGC):**

| # | Reporting Parameter |
|---|---|
| 1 | Number of stakeholders screened for mental health issues |
| 2 | Number of stakeholders diagnosed with mental health issues |
| 3 | Number of counselling sessions and outreach programs conducted |
| 4 | Number of referrals to external mental health professionals & services |
| 5 | Number of follow-ups of referred cases |
| 6 | Number of faculty-mentor sessions conducted, with the number of students |
| 7 | Number of peer support sessions conducted, with the number of students |
| 8 | Nature and frequency of crisis interventions (without personal identifiers) |
| 9 | Number of capacity-building programmes held with faculty, staff, and peer supporters |
| 10 | Feedback from students, staff, and families on well-being initiatives |
| 11 | Details of safety audits and infrastructure compliance |
| 12 | Number of awareness/sensitization sessions conducted for parents and community |
| 13 | Inclusion measures for marginalised and vulnerable groups |
| 14 | Institutional 24×7 Helpline and Emergency Contact (e.g. UGC Anti-Ragging Helpline, TeleMANAS, Women's Helpline) prominently displayed on the institution's website and within the campus, hostels, and other student services areas |

## 13. UGC — Digital Well-being Initiatives

*[Source: UGC Guidelines, Chapter 3, Section 3.6]*

- Institutes may establish an **Online Well-being Portal** linked with the UGC's MANAS-SETU
- The portal must offer:
  - Details of self-assessment tools and psychoeducation resources
  - Digital forms for reporting distress, harassment, or emotional difficulties
- **Partnership** of institutional 24×7 helpline with government helplines (TeleMANAS and other national helplines) for extended support after hours

## 14. NAAC Best Practice Format — Mental Health

*[Source: NAAC Health Sciences Manual, Key Indicator 7.2, Metric 7.2.1, p. 91]*

For institutions documenting their mental health initiative as an official **NAAC Best Practice**, the following NAAC-prescribed format must be followed:

### Format for Presentation of Best Practices (NAAC-prescribed)

**1. Title of the Practice**
This title should capture the keywords that describe the practice.

**2. Objectives of the Practice**
What are the objectives/intended outcomes of this "best practice" and what are the underlying principles or concepts of this practice? *(in about 100 words)*

**3. The Context**
What were the contextual features or challenging issues that needed to be addressed in designing and implementing this practice? *(in about 150 words)*

**4. The Practice**
Describe the practice and its uniqueness in the context of Indian higher education. What were the constraints/limitations, if any, faced? *(in about 400 words)*

**5. Evidence of Success**
Provide evidence of success such as performance against targets and benchmarks, review results. What do these results indicate? *(in about 200 words)*

**6. Problems Encountered and Resources Required**
Please identify the problems encountered and resources required to implement the practice. *(in about 150 words)*

**7. Notes (Optional)**
Please add any other information that may be relevant for adopting/implementing the Best Practice in other Institutions. *(in about 150 words)*

## 15. Glossary of Official Terms

*[Source: UGC Guidelines on Uniform Policy on Mental Health & Well-being for HEIs, Glossary of Terms, p. 7]*

| Term | Official Definition (as per UGC Guidelines) |
|---|---|
| **Mental Health** | A state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn and work well, and contribute to their community *(WHO definition)* |
| **Well-being** | A positive state and low levels of distress experienced by individuals and societies, along with overall good physical and mental health, a good quality of life, and the ability to engage and contribute to the world with a sense of meaning and purpose |
| **Resilience** | The process and outcome of successfully adapting to challenging life experiences through cognitive, psychological, and behavioural flexibility |
| **Life Skills** | Abilities for adaptive and positive behaviour that enable individuals to deal effectively with the demands and challenges of everyday life |
| **Early Identification & Intervention** | Mechanisms for recognizing signs of distress early and providing timely peer support and professional help for improved outcomes and building life skills |
| **Crisis Management** | Immediate, short-term, and coordinated actions to provide safety and scope for recovery in response to severe psychological distress, risk to self or others, or other emergencies |
| **Peer Support** | Structured student-to-student assistance through trained student mentors or volunteers to foster a sense of belongingness and to build life skills |
| **Mental Health & Well-being Centre (MHWBC)** | The institution's primary hub that provides mental health support and guidance, psychological services, crisis support, and develops awareness or promotion programs consistent with the emotional climate of the HEI campus |
