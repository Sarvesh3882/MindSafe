## WHAT IT MEASURES
The PHQ-9 screens for the presence and severity of Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
based directly on DSM-IV / DSM-5 diagnostic criteria.
It asks about symptoms over the past 2 weeks.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## OFFICIAL QUESTIONS (Exact Wording)
Instructions: "Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by any of the following problems?"
Response options: 0 = Not at all | 1 = Several days | 2 = More than half the days | 3 = Nearly every day

Q1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
Q2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
Q3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
Q4. Feeling tired or having little energy
Q5. Poor appetite or overeating
Q6. Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down
Q7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television
Q8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed?
      Or the opposite — being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual
Q9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way

Functional Impact Question (not scored):
Q10. If you checked off any problems, how difficult have these problems made it for you to do your work,
      take care of things at home, or get along with other people?
      [Not difficult at all / Somewhat difficult / Very difficult / Extremely difficult]

## SCORING
Total score = Sum of Q1–Q9 (Range: 0–27)

Score  | Severity Level       | Clinical Action
-------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------
0–4    | None / Minimal       | No action needed; monitor if needed
5–9    | Mild depression      | Watchful waiting; follow up in 1 month
10–14  | Moderate depression  | Treatment plan; consider therapy
15–19  | Moderately severe    | Active treatment (therapy / medication)
20–27  | Severe depression    | Urgent: immediate psychiatric evaluation

Diagnostic threshold: Score ≥10 → Sensitivity 88%, Specificity 88% for MDD
(Kroenke et al., 2001)

MDD Provisional Diagnosis Criteria (from PHQ-9):
- ≥5 symptoms present "more than half the days"
- At least one must be Q1 (anhedonia) or Q2 (depressed mood)
- ANY positive response on Q9 requires immediate follow-up

## PSYCHOMETRIC PROPERTIES
- Internal consistency (Cronbach's α): 0.89 (primary care), 0.86 (OB-GYN)
- Test-retest reliability: r = 0.84 (48-hour interval)
- Convergent validity: r = 0.73 with SF-20 mental health scale
- Used in 11,000+ scientific studies; translated into 100+ languages
- Unidimensionality confirmed in mega-study (n > 58,000; Bianchi et al., 2022)
- Endorsed by: USPSTF (Grade B screening recommendation), NICE (UK), WHO

## GLOBAL & INDIA EPIDEMIOLOGY
Global:
- 280 million people globally live with depression (WHO, 2023)
  Source: https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/depression
- Depression affects 3.8% of the world's population; 5% of adults; 6% of women; 4% of men
- Depression is the leading cause of disability worldwide (7.5% of all Years Lived with Disability)
- Fewer than 50% of people with depression receive effective treatment
  (In LMICs: fewer than 10% receive care)
- 700,000+ deaths by suicide annually are linked to depression
- By 2030: depression projected to be 2nd–3rd leading cause of disease burden in LMICs
  (GBD 2021 Study; Lancet Psychiatry, 2022)

India:
- 56 million Indians (4.5%) suffer from depression (WHO India estimate)
  Source: https://www.who.int/india/health-topics/depression
- 1 in 20 Indians suffers from depression (National Mental Health Survey 2015–16, NIMHANS)
- 197.3 million (14.6%) Indians affected by mental disorders (GBD Study, 2017)
- India's mental health disease burden doubled between 1990–2017
- 64.2% of suicides occur among those earning < ₹1 lakh/year
- Fewer than 30 million of ~200 million Indians who need care actually seek it
  (Source: PMC10793817)
- Only 0.75 psychiatrists per 100,000 people in India (WHO, 2021)

## KEY SIGNALS FOR SAATHI TO DETECT
(No direct PHQ-9 administration — pattern recognition only)

- Expressing loss of interest in things they used to enjoy
- Saying they feel "empty," "hopeless," "numb," or "what's the point"
- Mentioning sleep problems repeatedly (too much / too little)
- Reporting constant tiredness or low energy despite rest
- Self-critical language: "I'm a burden," "I'm a failure," "no one cares"
- Trouble focusing on work, studies, or daily tasks
- Withdrawing from friends, family, or activities
- Any mention of self-harm, death, or not wanting to be here

## SAATHI BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES

DO:
- Respond with warmth, not alarm
- Validate what they're feeling: "That sounds really exhausting."
- Ask open, gentle follow-up questions: "How long have you been feeling this way?"
- Suggest one small, manageable action (go outside for 5 minutes, drink water, text a friend)
- If signals are strong or persistent → gently mention professional support
- Mention Tele-MANAS (India): Free 24x7 helpline — dial 14416 or 1800-891-4416
- If Q9 signals appear (death / self-harm) → shift to safety-first response immediately
  (Refer to crisis_protocol.md)

DO NOT:
- Use the words "PHQ-9," "depression score," "clinically depressed"
- Catastrophize or amplify their pain
- Give unsolicited advice ("just exercise more," "think positive")
- Dismiss or minimize ("everyone feels sad sometimes")
- Ask multiple questions at once
